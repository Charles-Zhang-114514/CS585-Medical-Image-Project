Data Analysis Summary

During the process of training, the two diseases do not show up the same amount. Pleural Effusion 
occured 85,000 times during the training data. While Pneumothorax occured 20,000 times.

In the graphs, it appears that there are blank labels for Pneumothorax which was 144,000.For Pleural Effusion, there was 90,000. 

According to the validation data, there were 67 postive cases for Pleural Effusion while there was 8 positive cases for Pneumothorax. Based on this result, it appears it is difficult to find results from the model in regards to Pneumothorax.

The model does not show both diseases at the same time. Pneumothorax and Pleural Effusion both occur 6,691 during the training and occurs 2 times during validation. 

The x-rays are taken from a frontal view. There occurs to be 30,000 lateral views and 190,000 frontal views during the training of the model. Validation has more front views of 202 and lateral views of 31. 

Based on our findings, the data seems to differ. It appears that Pleural Effusion appears more often than Pneumothorax. The majority of x-rays are frontal view. Both diseases do not appear concurrently in the same validation data. With uneven data, it may impact the ability of the model to train. The model may not train as well.
